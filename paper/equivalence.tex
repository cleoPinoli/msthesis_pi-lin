\section{On the Equivalence of \LCC and \CP}
\label{sec:equivalence}

In this section we sketch in a more detailed way the relationship between \LCC
and \CP.

We take the forms $\Fork\x\y\z{P}{Q}$ and $\Join\x\y\z.P$

In \CP these processes are written as $\Fork\x\y{}{P}{Q}$ and $\Join\x\y{}.R$,
respectively, and their typing rules are formulated thus
\[
    \inferrule[\StandardForkRule]{
        \wtp{P}{\ContextC, y : A}
        \\
        \wtp{Q}{\ContextD, x : B}
    }{
        \wtp{\Fork\x\y{}{P}{Q}}{\ContextC, \ContextD, x : A \Ten B}
    }
    \qquad
    \inferrule[\StandardJoinRule]{
        \wtp{R}{\Context, y : A, x : B}
    }{
        \wtp{\Join\x\y{}.R}{\Context, x : A \Par B}
    }
\]
where the role of the continuation channel $z$ is being played by the session
endpoint $x$.

Every (well-typed) \LCC process

The equivalence between \CP and \LCC can be formally stated as an \emph{adequacy} result:

\newcommand\encode[1]{\varepsilon(#1)}
\newcommand\decode[1]{\delta(#1)}

\begin{align*}
    \encode{\Fork\x\y{}{P}{Q}} & = \Fork\x\y\x{\encode{P}}{\encode{Q}} &
    \encode{\Join\x\y{}.R} & = \Join\x\y\z.\encode{R} \\
    \decode{\Fork\x\y\z{P}{Q}} & = \Fork\x\y{}{\decode{P}}{\decode{Q\subst\z\x}} &
    \decode{\Join\x\y\z.R} & = \Join\x\y{}.\decode{R\subst\z\x}
\end{align*}

\begin{theorem}
    The following properties hold:
    \begin{itemize}
    \item If $\wtcp{\decode{P}}\Context$, then $\wtp{P}\Context$;
    \item If $\wtp{P}\Context$, then $\wtp{\decode{P}}\Context$.
    \end{itemize}
\end{theorem}
