\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\Cref{tab:comparison} summarizes a comparison among the formalizations of binary
session calculus that we are aware of, roughly sorted by publication date. We
have selected those formalizations that define a calculus of binary sessions
equipped with a reduction/evaluation semantics with proofs of subject reduction
and possibly of deadlock freedom.
%
The formalizations differ substantially in terms of reference calculus, used
proof assistant, process/expression/channel representation, handling of
linearity.
%
Most importantly, almost all of them predates the publlication of the linearity
challenge~\cite{CarboneEtAl24} hence were not necessarily targeted to solving
that particular problem. Nonetheless, they provide a broad spectrum of
techniques

\fullcite{GotoEtAl16} describe the oldest formalization of a session-based
calculus for which we were able to access the source code (the formalization of
\citet{Gay01} predates this work, but the source code is no longer publicly
available).

fullcite{RouvoetPoulsenKrebbersVisser20} also formalize a subset of \CP as case
study for their approach based on separation logic, which we have partially
taken inspiration from for the representation of processes
(\cref{sec:context-agda,sec:process-agda}). Their formalization adopts the same
intrinsically-typed representation of processes with nameless channels as our
own, but their formalization focuses on the implementation of an interpreter (no
